Optimizing LinkedIn Connection Management

Learn the efficient way to send follow-up messages and manage your LinkedIn connections like a pro!

Sending follow-up messages on LinkedIn can become a hassle, especially if you have a lot of connections. Since LinkedIn doesn't offer pagination, you're forced to scroll endlessly through your entire list. This not only takes up a lot of time, but on older devices, it can even cause the site to lag or freeze.

This quick guide will show you how to easily download your LinkedIn connection list, convert it into a clean spreadsheet, and remove unnecessary fields—using the help of AI to make everything faster and easier.

The Solution: Downloading LinkedIn Connections

To mitigate the scrolling issue, LinkedIn allows users to download their connections in CSV format. By downloading this data, users can quickly access, search, and organize their connections offline, eliminating the need to endlessly scroll.

  • Avoid the need for scrolling through large lists.
  • Have a permanent backup of your connections data.
  • Quickly sort and search through your list for more efficient management.

Offload data processing to your device or upload it to Google Docs to use online, reducing lag on LinkedIn. Work offline, eliminating the need for the browser to keep rendering large datasets.

Organize and filter your connections based on various factors (connection date, company name, etc.). Keep track of important connections or interactions without needing to scroll through the platform. Edit your file however you want.

Imagine downloading a large list of students and their data—say over a thousand items—to your computer. Once it's saved, you can open the full list instantly, scroll freely, mark important entries, remove what you don't need, or add custom notes and fields.

That's the kind of control and flexibility you get when you download your LinkedIn connections instead of dealing with endless in-app scrolling.

Advanced LinkedIn Automation Techniques

A step-by-step guide to download your LinkedIn connections data.

Navigate to your LinkedIn account settings and select "Settings & Privacy"

In the settings, look for the "Data Privacy" section. Select "Get a copy of you data".

From the available data categories, select "Connections" to download your full list of LinkedIn connections.

If this option doesn't exist due to constant policy change of LinkedIn, select to download a larger data archive, including connections, verifications, contacts, account history etc., and press "Request archive"

After submitting your request, LinkedIn will generate and send you a file—either a single CSV or a ZIP archive containing multiple CSV files. We are only interested in the Connections CSV file.

Once you extract the archive, you'll see a large number of CSV files. However, the only file we need is Connections.csv

Downloading your LinkedIn connections is similar to backing up contacts from your phone. Instead of endlessly scrolling through your list, you can download a file and have all your data saved in one accessible place.

Make your connection data easier to edit, filter, and use.

Once the CSV file containing your LinkedIn connections is downloaded, converting it to an XLSX file makes it easier to modify, filter, and organize your data using spreadsheet tools like Microsoft Excel or Google Sheets.

XLSX files are compatible with various spreadsheet tools, offering better functionality for sorting, filtering, and editing.
